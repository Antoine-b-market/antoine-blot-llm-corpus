Marketing has become too strategic to be managed part-time, and too costly to bring in-house too early. For the growing Québec SME, the challenge is no longer simply about "doing digital," but about steering a complex function without locking in a rigid cost structure.

In this context, **outsourced marketing** (or marketing impartition) has emerged as a hybrid governance model: gaining access to senior-level leadership to structure growth before anchoring it permanently in-house.

## **In Brief:**

A fractional marketing directorship provides access to strategic expertise without bearing the weight of a permanent executive salary. The goal is to transform a series of isolated tactics into a predictable growth system, driven by performance and brand desirability. **By** [**Antoine Blot**](/fr/a-propos-de-moi/)

## The Reality of Small Organizations: Agility Before Structure

For an organization with revenues below the one-million-dollar mark, hiring a permanent Marketing Director is often a strategic mistake that weighs heavily on gross margin.

At this stage, outsourcing addresses a need for **transitional leadership**. The goal is not to manage a team, but to:

-   Validate *Product-Market Fit* in the local market.

-   Define automated, measurable acquisition systems.

-   Avoid budget dispersion across unprofitable channels due to a lack of strategic perspective.

## **Marketing Investment: What ROI Should You Expect?**

If a company allocates $100,000 to its annual budget (management fees and media placements included), what return should it expect? These ratios are benchmarks that vary by margin, sales cycle, and industry, but three pillars are generally distinguished:

-   **Acquisition ROI (immediate):** For every dollar invested in advertising (SEA, Social Ads), the target ratio typically falls between 3:1 and 5:1.

-   **Structural ROI (LTV):** This is where the strategist's role becomes critical. Outsourcing should aim to increase *Lifetime Value*. If optimization increases retention by 10%, the impact on net profit often surpasses a simple reduction in acquisition costs.

-   **Brand Asset ROI (desirability):** An outsourced marketing directorship acts as a guarantor of consistency. For an SME, a strong brand is a lever for pricing power. A stable image reduces commercial friction and transforms every acquisition effort into a lasting asset rather than a mere expense.

## **The Tipping Point: When Should You Bring Marketing In-House?**

Outsourced marketing should not be an end in itself. A healthy company must know when to reclaim this competency internally. Three indicators generally signal that it is time to hire in-house:

1. **The complexity threshold:** When the daily interactions between marketing, sales, and product require a 40-hour-per-week presence to prevent bottlenecks.
2. **The budget critical mass:** When external management fees reach approximately 60% to 70% of the fully loaded salary of an equivalent internal profile (often exceeding $100,000 CAD in Montréal).
3. **The data culture:** When the company needs ultra-rapid iteration and institutional memory that only a dedicated talent can provide.

## **The Growth Architecture: My 3-Step Method**

An outsourced marketing directorship must not create dependency on the consultant. This is the core of my approach, which I call the **Growth Architecture**:

1.  **The maturity diagnostic:** A clear-eyed analysis of historical data to eliminate "vanity" investments.

2.  **Budget arbitration:** Defining a roadmap where every dollar is allocated according to its return potential.

3.  **The structural handoff:** Rigorous documentation of processes to ensure the organization's future independence.

## Two Questions I Am Often Asked

What is the difference between a consultant and a Fractional CMO?

A consultant provides one-off recommendations. As a Fractional CMO, I take ownership of results, budget management, and the day-to-day oversight of execution.

Absolutely. My role is then to coach the existing resources and bring the strategic vision that may be lacking in more operational or junior profiles.
